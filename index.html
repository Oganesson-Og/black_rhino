The Stones of Africa

African artisans work with a rich variety of stones, each carrying its own cultural significance and unique visual appeal. Verdite, known for its deep green hues, is one of the most cherished. Fruit serpentine, found in a spectrum of colors ranging from bright yellows and oranges to deep greens, is a favorite for its smooth texture and vibrant patterns. Opal, often associated with spiritual protection, offers stunning iridescent colors that give sculptures a mystical allure. Red jasper, a stone symbolizing strength and endurance, adds bold red tones to art pieces. These stones, along with springstone, lepidolite, and dolomite, have been used for centuries by Zimbabwean sculptors, creating enduring works that reflect the resilience and diversity of African heritage.

Its vibrant green hues symbolize growth and vitality, making it a perfect medium for capturing the essence of African wildlife.
